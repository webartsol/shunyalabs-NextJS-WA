Why This Matters

Bridging the gap in stuttering research

The Challenge

Stuttering affects over 80 million people globally, yet research has been fragmented. Every lab uses different annotation methods. Data can't be compared. Progress is slow.

The Solution

SAML creates a common language for stuttering annotation. When researchers, clinicians, and technologists speak the same language, breakthroughs happen faster.

of the world's population stutters, yet standardized research tools have been lacking

5x

Better data leads to better therapies, earlier intervention, and improved quality of life.

SAML is open source and free because access to good research tools shouldn't depend on your institution's budget. Whether you're at a major university or a small clinic, you deserve the same standards.

How It Works

Write naturally, export to standard format

Annotate in simple shorthand. The converter handles the technical details so you can focus on the data.

Shorthand Input 
I ( [R:isyl 1] w- w- ) was going to the store.
XML Output 
<p>I <saml:repetition type="incomplete-syllable"
    severity="1" count="2">
    <saml:unit>w</saml:unit>
    <saml:unit>w</saml:unit>
</saml:repetition>was going to the store.</p>
